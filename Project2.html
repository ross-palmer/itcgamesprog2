Following on from the work you have completed in lab 8, extend the tank demo to add the following functionality:

  1. Add capability for the AI tank to fire at the player tank. Add an AIState enum to the TankAI class as follows:

    enum class AIState { SEEK_PLAYER, FIND_HEALTH, FIND_COVER };

    These will represent the three states for the AI tank which will determine it's behaviour. The AI should start in SEEK_PLAYER mode, which means it will seek out the player tank. The AI should stop and fire when within a certain range of the player tank. The AI should always rotate it's turret to aim, unless the player tank is straight ahead. Add an appropriate delay between shots fired by the AI tank. [25]

  2. At predetermined intervals, spawn one health pickup at a random location on the map, but only on empty map areas. These pickups should stay on the map for a limited time before disappearing. Pickups should remain invisible until either tank is within a specific range of them. The HUD will show the pickup status (available/not available). Health pickups replenish the AI or player tank health by a specific amount [Note: you will need to assign a health property to both player and AI]. [20]

  3. The AI will switch to FIND_HEALTH state if it's health drops below a certain value. In this mode, the AI will seek out a health pickup if it knows one is available. Once the AI is within range of a pickup it will get its exact position and should seek to it. If none are available, the AI should switch state to FIND_COVER. [25]

  4. In FIND_COVER mode, the AI will seek cover from the player tank by positioning behind a wall (see if you can devise a good strategy for this). In the FIND_HEALTH and FIND_COVER modes, consider giving the AI a boosted top speed. Switch from FIND_COVER to FIND_HEALTH mode once a health pack becomes available. [20]

  5. The AI must show its current state on the HUD at all times. Also show the health of both tanks. [10]
