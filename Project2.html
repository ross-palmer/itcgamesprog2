  • Vision Cone Hints

For this project, you will need to implement a vision cone for an AI entity that can move independently around a simple level. You may continue using the Tank project from lab 8 or you may start with another suitable SFML project of your choosing. The gameplay involves the player trying collect an item from the opposite side of the map while avoiding the AI. The item needs to be brought to a drop-off point. The map has blockades which the AI and player have to navigate around. The player has a limited amount of fuel (if a vehicle) or stamina (if a person or some other life form). As the player moves, they deplete their fuel or stamina. The depletion rate is commensurate with movement speed. There is a small supply of fuel/food available as pickups to replenish fuel/stamina. The player has no way of damaging or destroying the AI. The game ends when the player has returned the item to the drop-off point or has been destroyed by the AI. The AI can only carry out ranged attacks (e.g. using projectiles), but has a limited firing rate. The projectile moves reasonably slowly so the player has a chance of avoiding them. However, the closer the AI is to the player, the less the chance the player has of avoiding projectiles. The player can move slightly faster than the AI. I suggest scaling down your sprite dimensions as a simple way of enlarging the playing area.

  1. Add a basic state machine to the AI to enable other behaviours. Add an AIState enum to your AI class as follows:

    enum class AIState
{
   PATROL_MAP,
   ATTACK_PLAYER
};

    These will represent the two states for the AI which will determine its behaviour. The AI has a vision cone which will enable it to see the player if it is 30 degrees left or right of its line-of-sight and within range of the cone. The AI will start in a tracking mode (PATROL_MAP) and will patrol random locations on the map looking for the player. In ATTACK_PLAYER mode, the vision cone has a longer range but narrower field of view, and does a 360 degree sweep of the map. The vision cone is always drawn and visible to the player. [30]

  2. Once the player is detected inside the cone, the AI switches modes to ATTACK_PLAYER and the following happens...

    2.1 The AI will proceed in the direction of the player and constantly measure the distance to that point, until it decides it is within a reasonable shooting range. The AI will no longer sweep the cone through 360 degrees, but will try to keep the player centered inside it. [20]

    2.2 If the player is approximately halfway inside the vision cone, the AI will adjust the cone, reducing its radius by about one third, but increase its spread to -40 and +40 degrees. [10] (note: AI will continue attempts to keep player centered inside the vision cone)

    2.3 If the player moves outside the vision cone, the AI will revert to the behaviour described for PATROL_MAP mode (this includes re-adjusting the vision cone). [10]

  3. Implementation of fuel/stamina and pickups as described above. If the player is hit by projectiles, this will initiate a constant slow fuel leak or reduction in stamina (in addition to the normal consumption while moving). There is a single repair pack/medpack available on the map to reset any damage. The player will be destroyed if they run out of fuel/stamina. [15]

  4. Use a HUD to show any relevant information (e.g. current fuel/stamina, current objective...). Use icons where appropriate. [10]

  5. Implement bounds checking on the map so the AI and player cannot leave the playing area. [5]

  6. Bonus: If you attempt this section, you can "top up" your project mark up to a maximum of 100. See if you can implement a system that prevents the AI vision cone from seeing through blockades (the cone does not have to be occluded by the obstacle, more so the player should not be spotted) [20]
